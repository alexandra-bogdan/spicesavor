Appetizer

Seared Scallops with Lemon Beurre Blanc Sauce and Microgreens

Ingredients:

  • 8 large sea scallops, patted dry
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Fresh microgreens or pea shoots, for garnish
  • Lemon slices, for garnish

Instructions:

1.Prepare the Scallop:
  • Season the scallops with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
  • Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until it starts to shimmer but not smoke.
2.Sear the Scallops:
  • Carefully add the scallops to the hot skillet, making sure not to overcrowd them. Sear the scallops for 1-2 minutes per side or until they develop a golden crust. Do not disturb the scallops while they are searing to ensure a nice crust forms. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
3.Make the Lemon Beurre Blanc Sauce:
  • In the same skillet, add the white wine and lemon juice. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and reduce the liquid by half, stirring occasionally.
  • Lower the heat and slowly whisk in the heavy cream. Let it simmer for a minute.
  • Reduce the heat to low and start adding the cold butter cubes, one at a time, while continuously whisking. Wait for each cube to melt and incorporate into the sauce before adding the next one. This process creates a creamy and velvety sauce.
  • Once all the butter is incorporated, add the lemon zest and season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste. Keep the sauce warm over very low heat, stirring occasionally.
4.Plate the Dish:
  • Arrange the seared scallops on serving plates.
  • Spoon the lemon beurre blanc sauce generously over and around the scallops.
5.Garnish and Serve:
  • Garnish the dish with fresh microgreens or pea shoots for a burst of color and freshness.
  • Add a slice of lemon on each plate for an elegant touch.
6.Serve Immediately:
  • Serve the seared scallops with lemon beurre blanc sauce immediately while they are hot.

Main Course

Herb-Crusted Salmon with Dijon Cream Sauce


Ingredients:

For the Herb-Crusted Salmon:
  • 2 salmon fillets (6-8 oz each), skin-on
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
For the Dijon Cream Sauce:
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

For the Herb-Crusted Salmon:
  • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Season the salmon fillets with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
  • Spread a thin layer of Dijon mustard evenly over the top (skinless) side of each fillet.
  • In a bowl, mix breadcrumbs, chopped parsley, chopped dill, and lemon zest.
  • Press the breadcrumb mixture onto the mustard-coated side of the salmon fillets, creating an herb crust.
  • Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Place the salmon fillets, crust side down, into the hot skillet and sear for 2-3 minutes until the crust is golden brown.
  • Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 10-12 minutes or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
For the Dijon Cream Sauce:
  • In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, Dijon mustard, and honey or maple syrup. Whisk together over medium heat until the sauce thickens slightly and becomes smooth.
  • Season the sauce with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Remove from heat.
To Serve:
  • Plate the herb-crusted salmon fillets.
  • Drizzle the Dijon cream sauce over the salmon.
  • Garnish with additional fresh herbs, such as dill or parsley, for a vibrant touch.
  • Serve the Herb-Crusted Salmon with Dijon Cream Sauce alongside your favorite fine dining sides, such as steamed vegetables, risotto, or a delicate salad.

Dessert

Chocolate Soufflé with Raspberry Sauce

Ingredients:

For the Chocolate Soufflé:
  • 4 ounces high-quality dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar, plus extra for coating ramekins
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
For the Raspberry Sauce:
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
For Garnish:
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting
  • Fresh raspberries and mint leaves, for decoration

Instructions:

For the Chocolate Soufflé:
  • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Butter the insides of 4 individual ramekins and coat them with granulated sugar, tapping out any excess sugar.
  • In a heatproof bowl, melt the dark chocolate and butter together using a double boiler or in the microwave in short bursts. Stir until smooth and let it cool slightly.
  • In a saucepan, heat the milk until it's hot but not boiling.
  • In another saucepan, whisk together flour and 1/4 cup granulated sugar. Gradually add hot milk, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  • Remove from heat and whisk in the melted chocolate-butter mixture and vanilla extract. Let it cool slightly.
  • Beat the egg yolks and add them to the chocolate mixture, stirring well.
  • In a clean, dry bowl, whip the egg whites with a pinch of salt until stiff peaks form.
  • Gently fold a third of the egg whites into the chocolate mixture to lighten it. Then, carefully fold in the remaining egg whites until no white streaks remain.
  • Divide the soufflé mixture among the prepared ramekins, filling them almost to the top.
  • Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the soufflés have risen and set but are still slightly soft in the center.
For the Raspberry Sauce:
  • In a blender, combine fresh raspberries, powdered sugar, and lemon juice. Blend until smooth.
  • Strain the raspberry sauce through a fine mesh sieve to remove the seeds, pressing down with a spoon to extract all the liquid.
To Serve:
  • Carefully dust the tops of the soufflés with powdered sugar using a sieve./li>
  • Create a well in the center of each soufflé and pour a spoonful of raspberry sauce into the well.
  • Garnish with fresh raspberries and mint leaves.
  • Serve the Chocolate Soufflé with Raspberry Sauce immediately while they are puffed and tempting.
