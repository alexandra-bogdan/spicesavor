Vegetarian Chickpea and Vegetable Stir-Fry

ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced (use any color you prefer)
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish (optional)
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions

1. Wash and chop all the vegetables as specified in the ingredients.

2. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, olive oil, rice vinegar, and maple syrup (or honey).

3. Heat a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add olive oil.

4. Add minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

5. Add broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and zucchini to the pan.

6. Stir-fry for 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

7. Add the chickpeas to the pan and stir to combine with the vegetables.

8. Pour the stir-fry sauce over the vegetables and chickpeas. Stir well to ensure everything is coated in the flavorful sauce.

9. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.

10. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions if desired.
